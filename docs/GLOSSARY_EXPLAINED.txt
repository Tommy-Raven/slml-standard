GLOSSARY EXPLAINED

This document explains glossary terms in plain language.
It is informative only.

The authoritative glossary is GLOSSARY.toml.

------------------------------------------------------------

Admissible

A manifest is admissible when it satisfies all required invariants and
passes validation.

Admissible does not mean endorsed or approved.
It means structurally consistent and validated.

------------------------------------------------------------

Corrupted

A manifest is corrupted when it violates an invariant or is incomplete.

Corrupted means “not permitted to claim compliance”.

------------------------------------------------------------

Manifest

A manifest is a machine-ingestible structured claim about a system.
It is not a guarantee.
It is a falsifiable assertion.

------------------------------------------------------------

Inconvenience

Inconvenience is declared burden imposed by a system.

Examples include time, cost, risk, or loss of agency.

------------------------------------------------------------

Obligation

An obligation is a requirement one entity imposes on another.

SLML restricts obligations that can be imposed on users.
